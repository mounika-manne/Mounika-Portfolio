About Me

I am Mounika Manne, a dynamic and self-motivated GIS and Water Resources Engineer with over 8 years of research experience. My academic journey encompasses a Ph.D. in Remote Sensing and GIS, an M.E. in Irrigation and Water Management, and a B.Tech in Civil Engineering.

Currently, I serve as the GIS Manager for Team India in the Tomkulak Consortium, an international non-profit organization. In this role, I manage and lead spatial data analysis for diverse projects, furthering sustainable solutions and environmental management efforts globally.

My expertise spans remote sensing data processing, GIS analysis, and ecological modeling. I am particularly passionate about developing innovative engineering solutions in environmental management, hydrological modeling, and water resource planning. My dedication to research and academia is demonstrated through my work at BITS Pilani and various industry projects, where I have applied sophisticated spatial analysis techniques to address environmental and water resource challenges.

My commitment to environmental science and sustainability drives my professional work and research, shaping the future of resource management and conservation.
